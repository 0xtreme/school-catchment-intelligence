How this works

Start here (for parents)
  1. Choose your state, then type a suburb or school name in search.
  2. Switch map metric to compare school quality, affordability context, or overall suitability score.
  3. Click any school dot to see plain-language metrics and what they mean.
  4. Use the charts to compare shortlisted schools against others in your filtered view.
Metric meanings in plain English
  • Overall suitability score: blended indicator combining quality context, affordability context, and staffing pressure.
  • School quality context (ICSEA): social-educational profile index; higher values often indicate stronger learning context.
  • Affordability percentile: higher values indicate more budget-friendly housing context around the school.
  • Class size pressure: students per teacher; lower values can indicate more teacher capacity per student.
  • SA2: ABS Statistical Area Level 2, a standard neighbourhood-sized region for local statistics.
Scoring model

The overall suitability score combines school quality context (ICSEA), housing affordability context (SA2 housing burden), and teaching-capacity proxy (inverse student-teacher ratio). It is designed for comparison, not as a single final decision.

Official sources used

  • NSW Department of Education catchments (Data NSW)
  • Victorian Government School Zones 2026 (DataVic)
  • Queensland school catchments 2025 (data.qld.gov.au)
